    CHOCOCLATE

    Recipe:

    In a bowl, mix together the cocoa powder and coconut oil until well combined. Add in the honey or maple syrup and mix until smooth. If desired, stir in any additional toppings like nuts or dried fruits. Pour the mixture into a chocolate mold or onto a lined baking sheet. Place in the refrigerator for about 1 hour, or until the chocolate is firm,Once firm, remove from the mold or break into pieces if using a baking sheet,Enjoy your homemade chocolate!.
    Ingredients:

    1 cup of cocoa powder, 1/2 cup of coconut oil,1/4 cup of honey or maple syrup, Optional: nuts, dried fruits, or other toppings.
    Calories:

    Almost 500-600 calories per 100gm.

    BISCUITS

    Recipe:

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C),In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt, Add the cold butter to the bowl and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut it into, the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs,Pour in the milk and stir until the dough comes together, Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together,Roll out the dough to about 1/2 inch (1.25 cm) thickness,Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out biscuits from the dough,Place the biscuits onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper,Bake in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown,Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.
    Ingredients:

    2 cups of all-purpose flour,1 tablespoon of baking powder,1/2 teaspoon of salt,1/2 cup of cold butter, cut into small pieces,3/4 cup of milk.
    Calories:

    Almost 400-500 calories per 100gm.

    POPCORNS

    Recipe:

    Heat a large pot or a stovetop popcorn popper over medium heat. Add the vegetable oil or melted butter to the pot and lup for a minute or two. Add the popcorn kernels to the pot and cover it with a lid. Shake the pot gently to coat the kernels with the oil or butter. Keep shaking the pot occasionally to prevent the poburning. As the kernels start popping, continue shaking the potpopping slows down. Once the popping slows to about 2 seconds between pops, pot from the heat. Let the popcorn sit for a minute to cool down and finish popping. If desired, sprinkle some salt over the popcorn and giveto distribute the salt evenly. Transfer the popcorn to a bowl and enjoy!.
    Ingredients:

    2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, 1/2 cup of cold butter, cut into small pieces,3/4 cup of milk.
    Calories:

    Almost 31 calories per 100gm.

    POTATO CHIPS

    Recipe:

    wash and peel the potatoes. You can leave the skin on if you prefer, Using a sharp knife or a mandoline slicer, thinly slice the potatoes into uniform rounds. Aim for slices that are about 1/8 inch thick,Place the potato slices in a bowl of cold water and let them soak for about 30 minutes. This helps remove excess starch and makes the chips crispier,Drain the potato slices and pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels,Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot to about 350°F (175°C),Carefully add a handful of potato slices to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan,Fry the potato slices for about 2-3 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and crispy,Usingslotted spoon or tongs, remove the chips from the oil and place them on a papertowel-lined plate to drain any excess oil,Sprinkle the chips with salt while they'restill warm, if desired,Repeat the frying process with the remaining potato slices until all the chips are cooked, Let the chips cool completely before serving to allow them become extra crispy..
    Ingredients:

    2-3 large potatoes,Vegetable oil for frying,Salt (optional).
    Calories:

    Almost 536 calories per 100gm.

    YOUGART

    Recipe:

    Pour the milk into a saucepan and heat it over medium heat until it reaches a temperature of about 180°F (82°C). Stir occasionally to prevent scorching.Once the milk reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the heat and let it cool down to about 110°F (43°C). You can use a food thermometer to check the temperature,In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of plain yogurt with a small amount of the cooled milk to create a smooth paste,Add the yogurt mixture back into the remaining cooled milk and stir well to combine, Pour the milk and yogurt mixture into clean glass jars or containers with lids,Cover the jars with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and place them in a warm spot, You can also use a yogurt maker if you have one,Let the yogurt ferment for about 6-8 hours, or until it reaches your desired level of tanginess and thickness, The longer it ferments, the tangier it will become,Once the yogurt is ready, refrigerate it for a few hours to chill and set,.
    Ingredients:

    1 liter of milk (any type, such as whole, low-fat, or plant-based), 2 tablespoons of plain yogurt with live active cultures (as a starter)
    Calories:

    Almost 61 calories per 100gm.

    Recipe:

    Ingredients:

    4-5 fresh apples,1 tablespoon of lemon juice (optional),Sweetener like honey or sugar (optional).
